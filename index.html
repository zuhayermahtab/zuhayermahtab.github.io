Current Research

The Ridesharing Routing Problem with Flexible Pickup and Drop-off Points

Developed a Branch-and-Price based approach for solving the ride-share routing problem with flexible pickup and drop-off points. The key innovation is an efficient algorithm for selecting optimal meeting points on a continuous plane, rather than using fixed discrete points. Testing on San Francisco Taxicab data showed that this approach can reduce total vehicle travel time by 4% and efficiently solve instances with up to 600 nodes.

Stochastic Rideshare System with Uncertain Travel Times and Passenger Cancellations

Proposed a data-driven stochastic rideshare system that handles uncertainties in traffic congestion and passenger cancellations. The approach uses historical data to learn travel time and cancellation patterns, rather than assuming probability distributions. Developed both a Branch-and-Price heuristic and an Adaptive Large Neighborhood Search-based metaheuristic, validated using New York City taxicab data.

Real-Time Large-Scale Ridesharing with Flexible Meeting Points

Designed an online, large-scale rideshare system featuring dynamic matching, flexible meeting points, and real-time incentive/cost-sharing mechanisms. The system includes request prediction and network simulation capabilities. Testing on New York Taxicab data and rural California datasets demonstrated significant cost reductions compared to traditional ride-hailing services and a 13% reduction in total vehicle miles traveled in Manhattan.

Projects

Airline Delay Analysis Dashboard

Created an interactive dashboard to analyze and visualize airline delay patterns and trends across 2023-2024.

Simulation Optimization of a Fortune-500 Manufacturing Company

Designed a simulation model of a complex manufacturing process and used it to optimize the production process by line balancing. The outputs of the simulation were analyzed to identify bottlenecks and improve critical KPIs such as utilization, queue times and throughput.

Simulation Optimization of a Dairy Plant Manufacturing Process

Gathered extensive data directly from the production floor of a dairy plant and used it to simulate the manufacturing process. The simulation model was used in conjunction with OptQuest (the simulation optimization toolbox) to maximize the production rate of UHT and pasteurized milk processes.
